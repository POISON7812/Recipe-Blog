Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • Khoya (Mawa) - 1 cup
  • All-Purpose Flour (Maida) - 2 tablespoons
  • Baking Powder - 1/4 teaspoon
  • Milk - 2-3 tablespoons (for kneading)
  • Ghee or Oil - for deep frying
  • Sugar - 1 1/2 cups
  • Water - 1 1/2 cups
  • Cardamom Pods - 4-5 (optional)
  • Rose Water - 1 teaspoon (optional)
  • Saffron Strands - a few (optional)
  • - 1 teaspoon (prevents crystallization)

Recipe

  1. Prepare the Dough: Mix 1 cup of khoya, 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour, and 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder. Knead with 2-3 tablespoons of milk into a smooth dough and form small, smooth balls.
  2. Make Sugar Syrup: Boil 1 1/2 cups of sugar with 1 1/2 cups of water, 4-5 cardamom pods, and a few saffron strands (optional). Add 1 teaspoon of lemon juice and 1 teaspoon of rose water. Simmer until slightly thickened.
  3. Heat Oil: Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan over medium-low heat for deep frying.
  4. Fry the Balls: Fry the dough balls until golden brown, ensuring they are evenly cooked. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Soak in Syrup: Soak the fried gulab jamuns in warm sugar syrup for at least 2 hours before serving.

Kaju Katli

Ingredients

  • Cashew Nuts (Kaju) - 1 cup
  • Sugar - 1/2 cup
  • Water - 1/4 cup
  • Ghee - 1 teaspoon (for greasing)
  • Cardamom Powder - 1/4 teaspoon (optional)
  • Saffron Strands - a few (optional)
  • Silver Vark - for decoration (optional)

Recipe

  1. Prepare Cashew Powder: Grind 1 cup of cashew nuts into a fine powder without over-grinding to avoid releasing oil.
  2. Make Sugar Syrup: In a pan, dissolve 1/2 cup of sugar in 1/4 cup of water over medium heat. Cook until it reaches a one-string consistency.
  3. Combine Ingredients: Add the cashew powder to the sugar syrup. Mix well and cook on low heat until it forms a dough-like consistency. Optionally, add 1/4 teaspoon of cardamom powder and a few saffron strands.
  4. Knead and Roll: Grease a surface with 1 teaspoon of ghee, transfer the dough, and knead it until smooth. Roll it out to about 1/4 inch thickness.
  5. Cut and Decorate: Cut into diamond shapes and, if desired, apply silver vark for decoration.

Jalebi

Ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour (Maida) - 1 cup
  • Yogurt (Dahi) - 2 tablespoons
  • Baking Powder - 1/4 teaspoon
  • Warm Water - as needed for batter consistency
  • Food Color - a pinch of yellow or orange (optional)
  • Sugar - 1 cup
  • Water - 1/2 cup
  • Saffron Strands - a few (optional)
  • Cardamom Powder - 1/4 teaspoon (optional)
  • Lemon Juice - 1 teaspoon (prevents crystallization)
  • Ghee or Oil - for deep frying

Recipe

  1. Prepare the Batter: Mix 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons of yogurt, and 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder. Gradually add warm water to form a smooth, thick batter. Optionally, add a pinch of yellow or orange food color. Let it ferment for 6-8 hours. aperiam, quos veniam sequi?
  2. Make Sugar Syrup: Boil 1 cup of sugar with 1/2 cup of water until it reaches a one-string consistency. Add a few saffron strands, 1/4 teaspoon of cardamom powder, and 1 teaspoon of lemon juice. Keep the syrup warm.
  3. Heat Oil: Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan over medium heat for deep frying.
  4. Pipe the Batter: Fill a piping bag or squeeze bottle with the fermented batter. Pipe spiral shapes directly into the hot oil. Fry until golden and crisp.
  5. Soak and Serve: Soak the fried jalebis in warm sugar syrup for about a minute, then remove and serve hot.

Rasgulla

Ingredients

  • Milk - 1 liter (full-fat)
  • Lemon Juice or Vinegar - 2-3 tablespoons (for curdling the milk)
  • Water - 4 cups (for the sugar syrup)
  • Sugar - 1 1/2 cups
  • Cardamom Powder - 1/4 teaspoon (optional)
  • Rose Water - 1 teaspoon (optional)

Recipe

  1. Prepare the Chhena: Boil 1 liter of milk and add 2-3 tablespoons of lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it. Strain the curdled milk through a muslin cloth, rinse with water, and squeeze out excess moisture to get chhena (paneer).
  2. Knead the Chhena: Knead the chhena until smooth and soft. Form small, smooth balls ensuring there are no cracks.
  3. Make Sugar Syrup: In a large pot, boil 1 1/2 cups of sugar with 4 cups of water. Add 1/4 teaspoon of cardamom powder (optional) and 1 teaspoon of rose water (optional).
  4. Cook the Rasgullas: Add the chhena balls to the boiling sugar syrup. Cover and cook on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes, ensuring the balls double in size and become spongy.
  5. Cool and Serve: Let the rasgullas cool in the syrup. Refrigerate for a few hours before serving chilled.
